Blown-In Attic Insulation

Fast, effective, and thorough, blown-in insulation fills your attic completely for consistent, reliable thermal performance.

What Is Blown-In Insulation?

Complete Attic Coverage in a Single Day

Blown-in insulation is one of the most efficient ways to insulate or re-insulate an attic. Using specialized equipment, loose-fill material is blown directly into the attic space, settling into every gap, corner, and hard-to-reach area. The result is a seamless blanket of insulation that eliminates cold spots and dramatically improves your home's energy efficiency, often in just a single day.

We use high-quality cellulose and fiberglass loose-fill materials, both of which conform to irregular attic spaces and work around wiring, pipes, and other obstructions. When installed to the correct depth, blown-in insulation brings your attic to the R-49 to R-60 range recommended for Minnesota's climate zone.

Complete Coverage

Loose-fill material conforms to irregular spaces, wiring, pipes, and framing without leaving gaps or voids.

Quick Installation

Most attic projects are completed in a single visit, with minimal disruption to your daily routine.

Cost-Effective Upgrade

Blown-in insulation offers excellent thermal performance at a lower cost compared to spray foam, making it ideal for attic applications.

Improves Existing Insulation

Can be added on top of old insulation to bring your attic up to current Minnesota energy code standards.

Reduces Ice Dams

A properly insulated attic keeps heat from escaping through the roof, which is the leading cause of ice dam formation in Minnesota.

Minnesota's Climate Challenge

Stop Losing Heat Through Your Roof

In Minnesota, heat rises straight through an under-insulated attic and out the roof. That means your furnace works harder, your energy bills climb, and your home stays drafty no matter how high you set the thermostat. Worse, that escaping heat melts snow on the roof, which refreezes at the eaves and creates ice dams, a common and costly problem across the state.

Blown-in attic insulation addresses all of this. It brings your attic to the recommended R-49 to R-60 range for Minnesota's climate zone, keeping heat where it belongs and preventing the cycle of energy waste and roof damage. If your home was built before 2000, there is a good chance your attic is significantly under-insulated. One day of work can make a measurable difference in your comfort and your heating bills for years to come.
