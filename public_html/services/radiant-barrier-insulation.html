Radiant Barrier Insulation

Reduce heat gain, improve indoor comfort, and lower cooling costs with professionally installed radiant barrier solutions.

Energy Control

Reflecting Heat Before It Enters Your Home

Radiant barriers are designed to reduce heat transfer caused by solar radiation, one of the primary sources of heat gain in homes. Unlike traditional insulation that slows heat flow, radiant barriers work by reflecting radiant heat away from living spaces before it can be absorbed.

Installed most commonly in attics, radiant barriers help keep homes cooler during warmer months by limiting the amount of heat that penetrates through the roof structure. This reduces attic temperatures and decreases the workload on air conditioning systems.

Crescent Moon Insulation installs radiant barrier systems as part of a broader energy-efficiency strategy, helping homeowners maintain more comfortable indoor temperatures while improving overall system performance.

Minnesota Homes

Reducing Solar Heat Gain Where It Starts

While radiant barriers are often associated with warmer climates, they can still play a valuable role in Minnesota homes by reducing heat buildup in attics during sunny periods. By limiting radiant heat transfer from the roof into the attic space, radiant barriers help stabilize indoor temperatures and improve cooling efficiency.

When combined with proper insulation and air sealing, radiant barriers contribute to a more balanced and energy-efficient home, particularly during peak summer conditions.
